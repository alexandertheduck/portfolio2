About Me

A hands-on engineer shaped by movement, manufacturing, and curiosity.

I grew up between Australia, Singapore, and Thailand, which gave me a practical sense of how different environments shape the way people work, communicate, and solve problems.

That mix of places also shaped how I think about engineering. I like understanding how things are made, why they fail, and how a rough idea becomes something reliable enough for real people to use.

Before university, I worked as a Manufacturing Technician in Thailand. Being close to machinery, fabrication, and day-to-day production gave me respect for the gap between a nice CAD model and a part that can actually be made well.

What drives me

Curiosity, hands-on learning, building things, solving real-world problems, and continuous improvement. I enjoy the full loop: observe the problem, design a response, prototype it, test it, and refine it until it earns trust.

At UNSW, I studied Mechanical and Manufacturing Engineering and graduated in 2025. My projects pulled me deeper into CAD, simulation, design optimisation, materials testing, and production thinking. During my engineering internship at Gate Gourmet, I developed a Discrete Event Simulation model to analyse bottlenecks and operational efficiency in catering workflows.

Today, I work as a Prototyping Engineer at KeenzKustoms. It is the kind of role I enjoy most: open-ended, practical, and close to the customer. A good day can move from CAD modelling to testing a prototype, solving a fabrication issue, or figuring out how to turn an unusual request into a finished product.
