Project

3D Printed Composite Research Thesis

Project Overview

Investigated carbon fibre reinforced nylon composites through mechanical testing, tensile strength evaluation, and analysis of how printed composite structures perform under load.

Engineering Tools Used

Additive manufacturingMechanical testingTensile analysisResearch writing

Problem

3D printed composites offer exciting manufacturing flexibility, but performance depends on material behaviour, print parameters, fibre reinforcement, and testing consistency.

Approach

Prepared specimens, evaluated tensile strength, compared results, and interpreted findings through a manufacturing and material-performance lens.

Challenges

Composite testing requires careful attention to repeatability, specimen quality, load paths, and the difference between theoretical strength and actual printed outcomes.

Results

Produced research findings around reinforced nylon performance and strengthened my understanding of material selection, test interpretation, and additive manufacturing constraints.

Image Gallery

Key Takeaways

Good engineering research depends on disciplined testing and honest interpretation. The project deepened my respect for how manufacturing process choices show up in final mechanical performance.
